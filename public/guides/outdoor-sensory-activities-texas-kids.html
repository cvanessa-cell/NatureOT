Outdoor sensory starter — Texas families

For caregivers. These ideas support exploration and regulation outdoors. Every child differs; pacing and adult supervision matter.

Educational only. This guide is not an occupational therapy evaluation, does not diagnose, and does not replace individualized care from a licensed provider.

Ideas to try

  1. Texture walk — barefoot or with water shoes on cool grass, smooth stone, bark (when safe).
  2. Nature scavenger relay — find soft, rough, fragrant, windy (movement) elements without collecting protected plants.
  3. Bucket pour play — controlled water pours between containers at a shady spot.
  4. Hammock sway break — short gentle vestibular input with caregiver present.
  5. Trail pacing game — walk fast / slow segments with clear start/stop cues.
  6. Shadow tracing — morning outlines on pavement with washable chalk.
  7. Zoo of sounds — imitate birds/insects sparingly (volume awareness for neighbors).
  8. Garden sniff & label — rosemary, citrus leaf (if edible/safe locally), mulch depth.
  9. Heavy work carry — small watered pots or soft bundles with weight limits suited to age.
  10. Cooldown routine — shade sit, slow breathing, hydrate, transition snack.

Heat & safety

  • Schedule around cooler hours; wear hats; check surface temperature.
  • Water, sunscreen, and shade breaks are non-negotiable in Texas sun.
  • Stop if a child shows distress; comfort first.
